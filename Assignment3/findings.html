Interesting Findings

The United States is responsible for nearly all coal consumption in North America, making up 93% of coal consumption in North America in 2012.
The US consumed roughly 83% of all power in North America.

In our dataset, North America only produced 1 metric ton per capita of C02 emissions in 1980. The following year, it jumped to 16.5. In the same dataset,
it shows that this figure fell to 0 for the years of 2004-2006.

Similar gaps in the data can be seen in both Africa and Europe. The CSV files show "N/A" for these years. I am not sure why this data wasn't collected.

North American coal consumption rose continuously up until 2007, whereas coal consumption in Europe has been decreasing since 1987.

Renewable energy consumption in the Middle East spiked dramatically from 2005 - 2007 before falling dramatically. It began to rise again in 2010.
